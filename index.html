Hello!

I am a PhD student in the Physics & Astronomy department at Johns Hopkins University, working with Floor Broekgaarden and Emanuele Berti. My research focuses on interpreting gravitational wave signals from the binary black hole merger population to learn about the lives, deaths, and interactions of massive stars across cosmic history.

The ability of a massive binary system to form a black hole binary that will merge within the age of the universe depends on the presence of heavy elements within the stars at birth. As a result, the merger rates and properties of the binary black hole population detected by gravitational wave detectors can be used to gain insight into the chemical enrichment and star formation history of the universe.

I use cosmological simulations of galaxy formation and evolution (IllustrisTNG) in combination with the binary population synthesis code COMPAS to probe the effects of the metallicity-dependent star formation history on the properties of the observable binary black hole population.
