Hello!

I am a first-year PhD student in the Physics & Astronomy department at Johns Hopkins University. I am advised by Floor Broekgaarden and Emanuele Berti.

I use the population synthesis code COMPAS (Compact Object Mergers: Population Astrophysics & Statistics) to evolve double compact object systems and model their merger rates and properties. I am particularly interested in how these properties evolve over cosmic time, and how they are affected by the characteristics of their progenitors (e.g. metallicity!).

In general, my research interests center around gravitational wave astrophysics, black hole and neutron star merger rates and properties, and binary and stellar evolution. I am interested in how gravitational waves can be used to study the properties of double compact objects and their progenitors in order to better understand stellar and binary evolution over cosmic history.
