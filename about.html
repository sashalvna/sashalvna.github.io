I did my undergraduate in Physics and Astronomy at Haverford College ('23), and began my PhD in the Physics & Astronomy department at Johns Hopkins University, where I earned my M.A. in Astrophysics. I transferred to UC San Diego at the end of my second year.

When I'm not thinking about stars, I spend my time creating art. My primary mediums are digital illustration (see a selected portfolio here) and fabric arts (sewing, crochet, needle felting). I am always learning, currently: knitting, beadwork, linocut printing, animation, and 3D modeling. Recently, I have been turning more towards physical media, as creating tangible art feels more and more critical with every day. As of now, my art is separate from my research; I am aiming to change that soon, though delving into astronomy visualizations.

I also enjoy growing my houseplant collection, visiting new coffee shops, thrifting, cooking, and I have picked up a love of tidepooling since moving to San Diego :)
